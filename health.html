Limitations of Retrospective Activity Tracking

Standard engagement models track lagging indicators and do not reliably mitigate acute physiological risk[cite: 362].

  • Activity Metrics: Incentivizing movement (e.g., step goals) without physiological context may elevate musculoskeletal injury risk during periods of accumulated tissue fatigue[cite: 365, 366].
  • Engagement vs. Claims: High utilization of general wellness portals has not demonstrated a reliable correlation with reduced severe medical loss ratios[cite: 372, 374].
  • Timing Deficit: Costs are typically identified post-event, when the liability is already incurred and clinical intervention is required[cite: 350].
Analysis refers to generic engagement architectures that operate without real-time biometric contextualization[cite: 362, 366].
